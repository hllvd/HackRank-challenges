We define a magic square to be an n x n matrix of distinct positive integers from 1 to n.sqrt(2) where the sum of any row, column, or diagonal of length n is always equal to the same number: the magic constant.

You will be given a 3 x 3 matrix s of integers in the inclusive range [1,9]. We can convert any digit a to any other digit b in the range [1,9] at cost of |a-b|. Given s, convert it into a magic square at minimal cost. Print this cost on a new line.
Note: The resulting magic square must contain distinct integers in the inclusive range [1,9].
Example
$s = [[5, 3, 4], [1, 5, 8], [6, 4, 2]]

The matrix looks like this:
5 3 4
1 5 8
6 4 2


We can convert it to the following magic square:
8 3 4
1 5 9
6 7 2


This took three replacements at a cost of
|5-8|+|8-9|+|4-7|=7
